The SCARF Project

The Simulating Clustered stAR Formation (SCARF) project is a suite of cosmological hydrodynamic simulations resolving the formation of star clusters in the early Universe.

Overview The Team Papers Visualization Data Access

Overview

Giant molecular clouds are the cradles of star clusters, in which most stars form, particularly in the early Universe. Understanding the modes and the efficiency with which stars and clusters form remains a central challenge in modern astrophysics.


Simulating clustered star formation in cosmic morning

We develop a continuous star cluster formation model in the cosmological hydrodynamic code ART to simulate the effects of clustered star formation during the cosmic morning (z = 3 - 10) on the observational properties of the earliest galaxies by the Hubble and James Webb Space Telescopes.
